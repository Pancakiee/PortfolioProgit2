Encapsulation:
Coding Aspect: Encapsulation is defined as the bundling of data and methods that operate on the data into a single unit, known as a class. This promotes data hiding and reduces the complexity of the code.
Daily Life Lesson: Encapsulation can be applied to personal organization. Similar to how a class encapsulates related data and methods, personal organization involves grouping related tasks, items, or responsibilities together.In a digital context, this could involve organizing your files, folders, or digital tools in a structured manner. Grouping related documents or applications together on your computer or device can help streamline your digital workflow. Encapsulation in personal organization leads to improved productivity and efficiency. It minimizes the time and effort spent searching for items or switching between tasks.

What are the new insights/discoveries that I learned?

Inheritance is one of the fundamental principles of OOP that allows a new class (subclass or derived class) to inherit properties and behaviors from an existing class (superclass or base class). The subclass can then extend or override these inherited attributes, promoting code reuse and creating a hierarchical relationship between classes. Inheritance allows a class to inherit properties and behaviors from another class. This means that a subclass can acquire the attributes and methods of its parent class without having to redefine them. The subclass automatically gains access to the fields and methods of the superclass.
